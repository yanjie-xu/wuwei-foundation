Remuneration

Voluntary governance

Board members perform their governance duties on a voluntary basis. They do not receive salaries, profit distributions, or attendance fees for their management work.

Reasonable and documented expenses incurred on behalf of the foundation may be reimbursed upon submission of appropriate supporting evidence.

Financial accountability

Responsible use of funds

Funds received by Stichting Wuwei Foundation are used exclusively to advance its charitable purposes and to cover reasonable necessary operating costs, including banking, compliance, administration, and website maintenance.

The first annual financial report will cover the relevant 2026 financial period and will be published after the close of the financial year within the applicable publication period.

The report will include, where required, a balance sheet, a statement of income and expenditure, and explanatory notes.

Grant decisions

Funding allocation policy

Stichting Wuwei Foundation operates an independent review process for charitable proposals submitted by third parties. Submission of an application does not guarantee approval or financial support.

The Board may approve, reject, postpone, or adjust a proposed grant based on available resources, project feasibility, governance, transparency, expected public benefit, and alignment with the foundation’s charitable purposes.

No entitlement to funding arises from the application process. Funding decisions are final, subject to the foundation’s articles, policies, and applicable law.

Monitoring

Compliance after funding

Approved grants and relief funds are subject to proportionate post-award monitoring. Funding agreements may require recipients to provide budgets, financial reports, receipts, project milestones, photographs, and other evidence of implementation.

The foundation may conduct document reviews, remote checks, or site visits where appropriate. Funding may be suspended or terminated if funds are used for unauthorised, commercial, misleading, or non-charitable purposes. The foundation may seek repayment where permitted by the funding agreement and applicable law.

Intellectual property

Clear rights, agreed in writing

Where a funded project creates educational, cultural, or well-being materials, the relevant grant agreement may give Stichting Wuwei Foundation permission to use those materials for non-profit communication, education, and training.

Ownership and licensing arrangements will be confirmed in writing with the project partner. The core philosophical frameworks and methodologies of the Wuwei Institute remain the intellectual property of their lawful owner.
